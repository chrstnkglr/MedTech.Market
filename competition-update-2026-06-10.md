## Philips Future Health Index 2026: AI is already saving clinicians time and delivering measurable impact in healthcare

**Author:** Royal Philips  
**Source:** [Philips](https://www.philips.com/a-w/about/news/archive/standard/news/press/2026/philips-future-health-index-2026-ai-is-already-saving-clinicians-time-and-delivering-measurable-impact-in-healthcare.html)  
**Publication date:** Jun 09, 2026

Philips published its Future Health Index 2026, reporting that AI is already delivering measurable operational benefits for clinicians, including reported time savings, expanded patient capacity and reduced work-related stress. The report also highlights barriers to scale, especially fragmented IT environments, limited interoperability and inadequate AI training. Strategically, Philips is framing AI less as a standalone product category and more as an enterprise transformation lever tied to productivity, staff capacity and clinical confidence.

### Strategic relevance
**Trend indicator / Opportunity:** The report supports Philips’ positioning around integrated informatics, AI-enabled workflow and enterprise-wide transformation. For competitors, the main implication is that AI differentiation will increasingly depend on demonstrable implementation outcomes, governance support and integration into existing clinical environments.

## Philips VeriSight Pro 3D ICE technology featured on BBC

**Author:** Royal Philips  
**Source:** [Philips](https://www.philips.com/a-w/about/news/archive/standard/news/articles/2026/philips-verisight-pro-3d-ice-technology-featured-on-bbc.html)  
**Publication date:** Jun 09, 2026

Philips highlighted BBC coverage of its VeriSight Pro 3D intracardiac echocardiography technology, emphasizing real-time 3D imaging from inside the heart for minimally invasive structural heart procedures. The communication positions the technology as an alternative to more invasive imaging approaches that may require general anesthesia, while linking it to Philips’ broader image-guided therapy portfolio, including Azurion and future AI-enabled guidance.

### Strategic relevance
**Observation / Risk:** Philips is reinforcing its procedural imaging narrative in structural heart care, where imaging quality, workflow simplification and therapy enablement are increasingly connected. This strengthens competitive pressure in image-guided therapy by tying modality innovation directly to procedure expansion and patient-throughput arguments.

## Medtronic set for Stealth AXiS expansion in Europe

**Author:** Susan Kelly  
**Source:** [MedTech Dive](https://www.medtechdive.com/news/medtronic-set-for-stealth-axis-expansion-in-europe/822369/)  
**Publication date:** June 9, 2026

Medtronic received CE mark coverage in Europe for Stealth AXiS in ear, nose and throat procedures, expanding beyond previously cleared spine and cranial indications. The platform integrates planning, navigation and robotics, and connects with Medtronic’s AiBLE ecosystem for AI, data and service-enabled planning and post-operative analysis. MedTech Dive also notes Medtronic’s view that the system can improve operating-room efficiency and strengthen installed-base stickiness.

### Strategic relevance
**Risk / Trend indicator:** Medtronic is extending a navigation and robotics platform across procedure categories, using regulatory expansion to broaden utilization. This is competitively relevant because image-guided intervention is moving toward integrated ecosystems that combine navigation, robotics, planning software, AI and service models.
