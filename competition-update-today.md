# Competition Update – MedTech Market – June 3, 2026

## tl;dr
- Philips received U.S. FDA 510(k) clearance for Elevate Plus, reinforcing the shift toward AI-enabled ultrasound workflow automation on premium installed platforms.
- United Imaging entered a U.S. theranostics collaboration with Telix, linking scanner hardware, software, AI and radiopharmaceutical workflows in molecular imaging.
- Subtle Medical raised $33 million to scale vendor-neutral AI image enhancement across MRI, PET and CT, strengthening the independent software layer around existing imaging fleets.
- Pulmera’s FDA-cleared CBeam platform targets a cost-efficiency angle by converting existing 2D surgical C-arms into intraoperative 3D imaging systems.
- Clarius added FDA-cleared AI for real-time ejection fraction assessment, showing continued AI migration into handheld and point-of-care ultrasound.
- Mindray expanded its Hepatus-Series for gastroenterology ultrasound, combining elastography, AI-assisted acquisition and enterprise data features.
- The common pattern is clear: competitors and challengers are positioning AI less as standalone interpretation and more as embedded workflow, utilization and productivity infrastructure.

## Philips receives FDA 510(k) clearance for Elevate Plus for EPIQ Elite and Affiniti, delivering AI advancements in general imaging ultrasound

**Author:** Royal Philips  
**Source:** [Philips](https://www.philips.com/a-w/about/news/archive/standard/news/press/2026/philips-receives-fda-510k-clearance-for-elevate-plus-for-epiq-elite-and-affiniti-delivering-ai-advancements-in-general-imaging-ultrasound.html)  
**Publication date:** June 2, 2026

Philips announced U.S. FDA 510(k) clearance for Elevate Plus on its EPIQ Elite and Affiniti ultrasound systems, alongside CE Mark status. The update adds AI and automation capabilities for routine general imaging workflows, including automated abdominal measurements, on-cart Koios decision support for breast and thyroid applications, image quality enhancements and remote upgradability. Philips positions the release around standardization, reduced repeat scanning and faster exams, with cited evaluation data indicating up to 30% scanning-time reduction in selected workflows.

**Strategic relevance:** **Risk / Trend indicator.** Philips is reinforcing a platform-upgrade strategy that protects premium ultrasound installed base value while adding AI workflow automation as a differentiator against GE HealthCare, Samsung Medison, Canon and Mindray. The integration of third-party Koios AI on-cart is also relevant because it shows Philips using ecosystem integration to make ultrasound consoles more clinically sticky.

## Telix and United Imaging Announce Strategic Theranostics Collaboration

**Author:** Telix Pharmaceuticals and United Imaging Healthcare North America  
**Source:** [Telix Pharmaceuticals](https://telixpharma.com/news-views/telix-and-united-imaging-announce-strategic-theranostics-collaboration/)  
**Publication date:** June 2, 2026

Telix and United Imaging signed a memorandum of understanding to evaluate a U.S.-focused strategic research collaboration in integrated theranostics. The collaboration pairs United Imaging scanner platforms, software, connectivity and AI capabilities with Telix’s molecular imaging portfolio and clinical protocols. Initial work will focus on TLX101-Px, Telix’s glioma PET imaging candidate, with efforts planned around protocol optimization, workflow support tools, validation and pilot programs.

**Strategic relevance:** **Risk / Opportunity.** United Imaging is moving beyond modality placement into workflow-level molecular imaging integration, an important competitive signal in PET/CT and theranostics. The partnership could strengthen United Imaging’s credibility in advanced oncology workflows if it translates into standardized, scalable implementations around radiopharmaceutical use.

## AI-powered medical imaging startup Subtle Medical picks up $33M and taps new CEO

**Author:** Heather Landi  
**Source:** [Fierce Healthcare](https://www.fiercehealthcare.com/health-tech/ai-powered-medical-imaging-startup-subtle-medical-picks-33m-and-taps-new-ceo)  
**Publication date:** June 2, 2026

Subtle Medical raised $33 million in Series C growth capital led by Morgan Stanley Expansion Capital and appointed Ohad Arazi as CEO. The company plans to expand product development and commercial adoption of its vendor-neutral AI imaging platform across MRI, PET and CT workflows. Subtle’s positioning centers on improving image quality and reducing scan times on existing scanners, with reported deployment on more than 1,300 scanners and 11 FDA clearances.

**Strategic relevance:** **Risk / Trend indicator.** Vendor-neutral AI image enhancement remains a competitive pressure point for OEMs because it can deliver throughput gains without full scanner replacement. For major imaging vendors, Subtle’s growth reinforces the need to either outperform independent AI layers natively or partner where customers prioritize cross-fleet standardization.

## Pulmera wins FDA clearance for 3D imaging platform

**Author:** Sean Whooley  
**Source:** [MassDevice](https://www.massdevice.com/pulmera-wins-fda-clearance-for-3d-imaging-platform/)  
**Publication date:** June 2, 2026

Pulmera received FDA 510(k) clearance for CBeam, a 3D intraoperative imaging platform designed to attach to conventional 2D surgical C-arms. The system captures a rotational X-ray image series and reconstructs a 3D dataset for orthopedic and neurological procedures. Pulmera emphasizes the ability to expand access to intraoperative 3D visualization using existing C-arm infrastructure.

**Strategic relevance:** **Risk / Observation.** CBeam targets the same value pool as premium intraoperative 3D and cone-beam imaging systems but with a lower-capex, infrastructure-reuse proposition. If clinically adopted, this type of retrofit model could pressure high-end surgical imaging differentiation in cost-sensitive hospitals and ambulatory surgery centers.

## Clarius gets FDA clearance for ejection fraction AI tool

**Author:** AuntMinnie.com staff writers  
**Source:** [AuntMinnie](https://www.auntminnie.com/clinical-news/ultrasound/news/15826589/clarius-mobile-health-clarius-gets-fda-clearance-for-ejection-fraction-ai-tool)  
**Publication date:** June 2, 2026

Clarius Mobile Health received FDA 510(k) clearance for Ejection Fraction AI, a semi-automated LVEF measurement tool integrated into the Clarius Ultrasound App. The tool calculates and displays ejection fraction in real time from standard cardiac ultrasound windows and is available for users of selected Clarius wireless ultrasound scanners.

**Strategic relevance:** **Trend indicator.** AI-enabled quantification is continuing to move into handheld ultrasound, broadening competitive pressure from cart-based systems into point-of-care and primary care settings. This supports a market pattern in which workflow automation and guided measurements become baseline expectations even outside traditional radiology and cardiology environments.

## Mindray Leads the Evolution of Gastroenterology Imaging with the Newly Expanded Hepatus-Series Ultrasound Systems

**Author:** Mindray North America  
**Source:** [PR Newswire](https://www.streetinsider.com/PRNewswire/Mindray%2BLeads%2Bthe%2BEvolution%2Bof%2BGastroenterology%2BImaging%2Bwith%2Bthe%2BNewly%2BExpanded%2BHepatus-Series%2BUltrasound%2BSystems/26589659.html)  
**Publication date:** June 2, 2026

Mindray launched an upgraded Hepatus-Series for gastroenterology imaging, including Hepatus 6 as a broader GI imaging platform and Hepatus 5 as a liver-focused system. The series integrates visualized transient elastography with diagnostic ultrasound for liver, bowel and spleen assessment. Mindray also highlighted AI-powered Q-Scan Intelligent Acquisition, real-time quality control feedback, structured data capture and software upgrade pathways.

**Strategic relevance:** **Risk / Observation.** Mindray is using specialty workflow depth, AI-assisted acquisition and long-life platform economics to compete in ultrasound beyond general imaging. The move is particularly relevant for liver and GI care pathways, where integrated elastography and ultrasound can support service-line expansion in outpatient and specialty settings.
