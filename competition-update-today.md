# Competition Update – MedTech Market – June 1, 2026

## tl;dr
United Imaging continued to use SNMMI 2026 to strengthen its molecular imaging positioning, adding compact PET/CT and PET/MR updates around workflow, scalability and theranostics. The move reinforces a competitive pattern: challengers are broadening portfolios not only through scanner specifications, but through workflow automation, axial-field-of-view options and AI-supported reconstruction. Molecular imaging remains strategically active, with radiopharmaceutical therapy data from MTTI and ITM pointing to intensifying competition around theranostics pathways. These developments matter because scanner vendors, software players and radiopharmaceutical companies are increasingly competing for influence over the full diagnosis-to-treatment continuum. The near-term competitive signal is strongest in PET/CT, PET/MR and theranostics, where clinical capacity, protocol efficiency and therapy-readiness are becoming differentiators. No new dated, competition-relevant news was validated within the window for GE HealthCare, Philips, Canon Medical or Samsung Medison beyond already covered topics or undated event pages.

## United Imaging adds compact PET/CT to uMI Panvivo line

Author: AuntMinnie.com staff writers  
Source: [AuntMinnie](https://www.auntminnie.com/resources/conference/snmmi/2026/news/15826416/united-imaging-united-imaging-adds-compact-petct-to-umi-panvivo-line)  
Publication date: May 31, 2026

United Imaging expanded its uMI Panvivo molecular imaging PET/CT portfolio with uMI Panvivo Mobile, a compact PET/CT system. The system can be paired with automated clinical workflow and either an 80-slice CT configuration, which is pending U.S. FDA 510(k) clearance, or a 160-slice CT configuration for cardiac and general imaging. The company also highlighted the uMI Panorama GS long-axial-field-of-view PET/CT and the uPMR Zenith PET/MR system for oncology, neurology, drug development and theranostics applications; the PET/MR system is also pending U.S. FDA clearance.

**Strategic relevance:** Trend indicator / Competitive risk. United Imaging is pushing a broader molecular imaging platform strategy that combines scanner breadth, workflow automation and theranostics positioning. This strengthens its ability to compete against established PET/CT and PET/MR vendors by addressing both premium-performance and space-efficient deployment needs.

## MTTI highlights two-cycle EBTATE data for neuroendocrine tumors

Author: AuntMinnie.com staff writers  
Source: [AuntMinnie](https://www.auntminnie.com/resources/conference/snmmi/2026/news/15826415/mtti-highlights-twocycle-ebtate-data-for-neuroendocrine-tumors)  
Publication date: May 31, 2026

Molecular Targeting Technologies reported SNMMI data for lutetium-177 DOTA-EB-TATE in gastroenteropancreatic neuroendocrine tumors. The reported phase 1 data indicated materially higher tumor uptake than conventional Lu-177 DOTATATE and suggested potential clinical responses after fewer treatment cycles, while the company reported no kidney toxicity at one-year follow-up in the study population.

**Strategic relevance:** Trend indicator. Although early-stage, the data support continued innovation pressure in radiopharmaceutical therapy, especially around treatment efficiency, dosimetry and organ-sparing profiles. For imaging vendors, this reinforces the need to align PET/CT, SPECT/CT, dosimetry software and workflow tools with expanding theranostics use cases.

## ASCO: Lu-177 edotreotide yields sustained quality-of-life gains

Author: Erik L. Ridley  
Source: [AuntMinnie](https://www.auntminnie.com/clinical-news/molecular-imaging/nuclear-medicine/article/15826418/asco-lu177-edotreotide-yields-sustained-qualityoflife-gain)  
Publication date: May 31, 2026

ITM’s Lu-177 edotreotide showed sustained patient-reported quality-of-life benefit versus everolimus in a subset analysis of the phase III COMPETE trial in gastroenteropancreatic neuroendocrine tumors. The article notes that ITM has an FDA PDUFA date of August 28, 2026 for ITM-11, making this a commercially relevant late-stage radiopharmaceutical therapy asset.

**Strategic relevance:** Opportunity / Trend indicator. The data add competitive weight to the theranostics market and may increase demand for integrated molecular imaging capacity, patient selection, treatment planning and follow-up workflows. Vendors with strong PET/CT, SPECT/CT, quantitative imaging and enterprise workflow capabilities are better positioned to support providers scaling radiopharmaceutical therapy programs.
