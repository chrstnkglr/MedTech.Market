## Synthesize Realistic 3D Medical Images at Scale to Ship Pre‑Trained Models

**Author:** Can Zhao, Ibrahim Ethem Hamamcı, Marc Edgar and Monty Zarrouk  
**Source:** [NVIDIA Technical Blog](https://developer.nvidia.com/blog/?p=116517)  
**Publication date:** May 22, 2026

NVIDIA introduced NV-Generate-CTMR and NV-Generate-MR-Brain capabilities based on its MAISI architecture family, focused on generating realistic 3D CT and MRI volumes with paired segmentations. The stated objective is to address a key bottleneck in medical imaging AI: limited access to high-quality, annotated, privacy-compliant 3D imaging datasets. The framework is positioned as open and scalable for researchers and developers, enabling synthetic data generation to support training pipelines and pre-trained medical imaging models.

**Strategic relevance:**  
**Trend indicator / Risk.** Synthetic medical imaging data could lower barriers for AI entrants and accelerate model iteration outside traditional scanner-vendor ecosystems. For established imaging vendors, this reinforces the strategic need to differentiate through proprietary clinical workflow integration, validation data, regulatory pathways and installed-base access rather than model development alone.

## Lunit Highlights AI-Powered IHC and Tumor Microenvironment Research at ASCO 2026

**Author:** Yousub Lee  
**Source:** [Lunit](https://www.lunit.io/en/media-hub/lunit-highlights-ai-powered-ihc-and-tumor-microenvironment-research-at-asco-2026/)  
**Publication date:** May 22, 2026

Lunit announced five ASCO 2026 studies using its AI biomarker platforms for HER2/IHC quantification and spatial tumor microenvironment analysis across biliary tract cancer, NSCLC, adenoid cystic carcinoma, metastatic colorectal cancer and advanced gastric cancer. The announcement highlights expansion from conventional immune profiling toward integrated biomarker analysis using whole-slide image analytics. The studies position Lunit’s pathology AI as a tool for therapy-response stratification and precision oncology research.

**Strategic relevance:**  
**Opportunity / Trend indicator.** Lunit’s expansion shows how AI competitors are moving beyond radiology triage and detection into biomarker-rich oncology workflows. This increases competitive pressure around enterprise imaging and pathology integration, especially where imaging, digital pathology and therapy-selection data converge into platform-level clinical decision support.
