## GE HealthCare Submits Modeling Software for FDA Approval

**Author:** Not specified  
**Source:** [Imaging Technology News](https://www.itnonline.com/content/ge-healthcare-submits-modeling-software-fda-approval)  
**Publication date:** May 29, 2026

GE HealthCare announced that MIM KineticID, a software solution for dynamic PET workflows and kinetic modeling, is 510(k)-pending with the U.S. FDA. The software is designed to support quantitative assessment of radiotracer biodistribution over time, including 4D visualization, Patlak analysis, customizable curve fitting, automated labeling, multi-tracer comparison, and DICOM support across PET systems.

The competitive relevance is high because GE HealthCare is extending its molecular imaging proposition into disease quantification and theranostics workflow software. This strengthens the strategic value of the MIM acquisition by positioning GE not only around PET/SPECT hardware, but also around the interpretation, longitudinal assessment, and therapy-planning data layer.

**Strategic relevance:**  
**Risk / Trend indicator.** GE HealthCare is building competitive leverage in vendor-neutral molecular imaging software, which may increase account stickiness across PET, theranostics, and radiopharmaceutical therapy workflows even where the scanner fleet is mixed-vendor.

## UPMC Italy and United Imaging Healthcare bring Europe's first CE-marked uRT-linac 506c to Italy

**Author:** United Imaging Healthcare Europe and UPMC Italy  
**Source:** [PR Newswire via ADVFN](https://www.advfn.com/stock-market/stock-news/98632722/upmc-italy-and-united-imaging-healthcare-bring-eur)  
**Publication date:** May 29, 2026

UPMC Italy and United Imaging Healthcare Europe announced a partnership to install Europe’s first CE-marked uRT-linac 506c in Italy. The system integrates a diagnostic-quality CT scanner with a linear accelerator on a single platform, enabling CT image guidance immediately before treatment, online adaptive planning, automated contouring, automated treatment planning, and AI-supported workflow execution.

This marks a strategically relevant European deployment for United Imaging in radiotherapy, a segment where the company is trying to extend its imaging base into treatment delivery. The announcement also indicates a commercial move from product presentation at ESTRO toward clinical adoption with a recognized international healthcare provider.

**Strategic relevance:**  
**Risk / Opportunity.** United Imaging is using integrated imaging-treatment architecture and native AI workflow claims to challenge established radiotherapy and image-guided therapy players in Europe. The near-term risk is strongest in accounts prioritizing adaptive radiotherapy efficiency and integrated platform economics.

## SKIA HEAD: AR Surgical Guidance Receives FDA 510(k) Clearance, Eyes U.S. Expansion with Structure Partnership

**Author:** SKIA  
**Source:** [PR Newswire](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/skia-head-ar-surgical-guidance-receives-fda-510k-clearance-eyes-us-expansion-with-structure-partnership-302785511.html)  
**Publication date:** May 29, 2026

South Korea-based SKIA received FDA 510(k) clearance for SKIA HEAD, a tablet-based augmented reality surgical guidance system. The platform converts preoperative medical imaging data into 3D anatomical reconstructions and projects them onto the patient in real time. SKIA also announced a U.S. launch plan supported by a hardware partnership with Structure.

The development is relevant as a sign of continued decentralization and simplification in image-guided surgery. While not a direct modality competitor, SKIA’s approach competes for workflow value around preoperative imaging data, navigation, and surgical visualization, potentially challenging larger installed navigation platforms in selected use cases.

**Strategic relevance:**  
**Observation / Trend indicator.** FDA-cleared, portable AR guidance solutions may increase pressure on established surgical navigation and advanced visualization vendors, especially where customers seek lower-footprint alternatives to fixed navigation infrastructure.

## Nucs AI and AstraZeneca pursue new response-prediction models

**Author:** AuntMinnie.com staff writers  
**Source:** [AuntMinnie](https://www.auntminnie.com/clinical-news/molecular-imaging/news/15826345/nucs-ai-and-astrazeneca-pursue-new-responseprediction-models)  
**Publication date:** May 29, 2026

Nucs AI and AstraZeneca are collaborating to develop predictive image-based AI algorithms for PSMA-targeted radioconjugate research in metastatic prostate cancer. The collaboration aims to move patient selection from population-level eligibility toward individualized response prediction and to adapt AI models to next-generation radioconjugates.

This is competition-relevant because it shows pharmaceutical companies increasingly embedding imaging AI into therapy development and patient stratification. For imaging vendors, this reinforces the need to support quantitative, interoperable, and trial-ready molecular imaging workflows that can plug into radiopharmaceutical development ecosystems.

**Strategic relevance:**  
**Trend indicator.** AI-based response prediction is becoming a strategic layer in theranostics, with pharma partnerships potentially shaping future requirements for PET analytics, data management, and therapy-response software.

## Shine secures new European clearance

**Author:** AuntMinnie.com staff writers  
**Source:** [AuntMinnie](https://www.auntminnie.com/clinical-news/molecular-imaging/news/15826361/shine-secures-new-european-clearance)  
**Publication date:** May 29, 2026

Shine received European Medicines Agency marketing authorization for Ilumira, its non-carrier-added lutetium-177 radiopharmaceutical precursor. The authorization opens access to the EU market for use in targeted radioligand therapy development and follows several recent company milestones, including a large financing round, a U.S. Department of Energy conditional loan commitment, and acquisition of a U.S. isotope manufacturing and distribution operation.

The competitive relevance is indirect but material: isotope supply and precursor availability are becoming strategic constraints and differentiators in molecular imaging and theranostics. Expanded Lu-177 supply capacity can accelerate radioligand therapy development, which in turn increases demand for PET/SPECT imaging, dosimetry, therapy planning, and response-assessment workflows.

**Strategic relevance:**  
**Opportunity / Trend indicator.** Strengthening radiopharmaceutical supply infrastructure supports growth in theranostics and molecular imaging ecosystems, creating downstream demand for quantitative imaging, dosimetry, and integrated oncology workflow platforms.
